White Space

Ebay

Ebay.com

White space, the unmarked space between elements, increases the visual appearance of a webpage. It reduces clutter and a constricted look, which improves a user's understanding of the page.

Ebay's website is a great example of white space. All the elements have plenty of space away from one another, making the website look clean and not overwhelming to the user.

Fitts' Law

Google

Google.com

Fitt's law is a way of predicting human movement, usually when interacting with computer interfaces. It shows that distance and the size of a given target play a role in the amount of time required for someone to reach to the target.

In website design, this is very important to consider because choosing a design that supports easy and natural human movement will greatly improve the experieunce for the user.

Google's website is a good example of this because it shows a large search bar in the middle, making it very quick for the user to access Google's main purpose.

Visual Hierarchy

Pinterest

Pinterest.com

Another principle to obtain a good design is visual hierarchy. It's used to rank elements of a webpage by changing their aspects such as size, color, contrast, or position to each other. It improves the design and fluidity of a webpage.

Pinterest's welcome page is a great example of visual hierarchy because it uses colors and diferent contrasts to make different aspects of the page pop. The most important area, the login buttons, are bright and colored while the background is shadowed. This helps keep the page simple and intuitive.
